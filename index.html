The Wedge Play: Your Path to PLG

Even complex enterprise products can find a PLG entry point. Datadog sells to DevOps (enterprise buyer), but started with a simple infrastructure monitoring agent that developers could install themselves. Slack started as a team tool before becoming enterprise software.

Your play: Find the simplest, most self-serve use case within your product. Let that be your wedge into the organization. Once users love it, sales can expand the relationship.

Pure PLG

User and buyer are identical. No approval needed. Credit card swipe completes the sale. Focus on activation metrics and time-to-value.

Product-Led Sales

Let users experience value, then sales assists with team/department expansion. PQLs (Product Qualified Leads) matter more than MQLs.

Sales-Led

Multiple stakeholders, long procurement cycles, security reviews. Sales builds relationships and navigates the organization.
